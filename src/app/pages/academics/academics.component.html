Our Curriculum & Programs

At Holy Redeemer's Higher Secondary School, we offer a comprehensive curriculum designed to foster intellectual growth and critical thinking. Explore our diverse programs tailored to meet the needs of every student.

Rules & Regulations

School Work Order

1. School has two working sessions, from 9.00am to 12.30pm and from 1.30pm to 4.20pm.

2. The first bell goes at 9.05am. Assembly will be held at 9.05am, followed by a common prayer. Students should participate in both.

3. While gathering for the assembly and dispersing, silence and proper line should be kept up.

4. On all working days, from 9.00 to 9.30, catechism and moral classes are held for catholics and non Catholics respectively.

5. The morning break is between 11.00 and 11.10 am and the evening break is between 3.00 to 3.10 pm.

6. From 4.20 to 5.30 in the evening is the time for evening games. Students are encouraged to take part in it actively.

7. If the attendance of the high school students to games falls below 75%, they become unqualified for promotion.

8. If a student owing to health reasons needs to abstaint himself from the physical education class, he has to produce a medical certificate from the a recoganised physician.

9. At 4.30 pm, on all school days, the following are held:

Monday - The Scout and Guides.

Tuesday - NSS

Wensday - Litrary association

Thursday - Junior Red Cross

Friday - RRC

10. For the slow learners of X and XII students, special evening supervised study is conducted from 4.30 to 5.30 pm.

Leave

11. Students who request leave for foreseeable reason like marriage and festival, should get prior permission from the headmaster. Leave letters sent through other students will summarily be rejected.

12. Students requesting holidays due to illness and who have not got the HM's prior permission, should send their leave letters to the HM with the signature of the parent or warden.

13. Students will be permitted to attend the classes only ofter obtaining a letter from the HM, when they come after holidays.

14. Strict disciplinary action will be taken against those who are continuously absent without proper reasons.

Examination

15. Three major examinations are held every year.

16. Besides these, three mid term tests are held in the months of July, November, and February.

17.There will be prize distribution ceremony to let the parents know of how much their children secured in Quaterly, half-yearly exams. Parents should attend the function without fail and sign their childern's progress reports. Marks secured in the mid -term exams will be entered in the report card and sent to the parents.

Uniform

18. Students should come in uniform every day.

19. Badge is the part of the uniform.

20. Students should wear uniform even on exam days in the class.

21. Students should not leave the class without the permission of the teacher. To go out of the class hours, the permission of the HM is required.

22. If a student is not found in his place while the attendance will be consiter absent.

23. If a student comes late to the school he/she needs to get the signature of the HM to enter the class.

24. Visitors are not welcome during the class hours.

25. All the students should stand up when the teacher enters the class and they should sit only when the teacher asks them to do so.

On Campus

26. Students should take pride in keeping the campus clean. Every one should contribute his/her mite for class room cleaniness, and safety of the furnitures.

27. Students should throw the wastes into the dustbin only and keep the school campus always clean.

28. Wasting and dirtying the water and dipping plates inside the water tubs should be avoided.

29. Students should avoid growing long hair.

30. Students locked and kept in the allotted places.

31. Classrooms should not be used for taking food and students should use only the allotted places.

General Conduct

32. Nobody is allowed to get donations from students without the knowledge of the Correspondent.

33. Catholic students and teachers are expected to visit the Blessed Sacrament at 9.05am everyday.

34. It is the duty of students to greet teachers and school authorities when students meet them for the first time in a day.

35. Private tuition, if needed, should be arranged with the permission of the Headmaster.

36. Letters addressed to students will be scrutinized by school authorities.

37. There is a very good library in the school to enhance the general knowledge of the students. Students are encouraged to utilize the library.

A Request to Parents

In addition to imparting education, their innate talents are unearthed and channelized through extracurricular activities through sports & games. Thus their thinking capacity is made enhanced and they become men of action, consequently men for others.
